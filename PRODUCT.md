# Product

## Register

brand

## Platform

web

## Users

**Primary:** Self-studying professionals and students preparing for Brazilian certifications (IT, healthcare, finance, law, engineering) and concursos públicos. They open CertifiqueAI during commutes, after work, or on weekends — time is scarce and every study session needs to deliver real exam readiness. Secondary surface: the same user who just upgraded looks to the workspace as a focused training tool, not a content library.

## Product Purpose

CertifiqueAI generates AI-powered practice questions that match the style, depth, and specificity of real exams — not generic study cards. It exists because candidates can't always find quality, domain-specific practice material for the precise certification or concurso they're targeting. Success looks like: a user configures their certification, generates a focused question set, runs a simulado, reviews per-option AI explanations, and leaves the session meaningfully more prepared than when they arrived.

## Positioning

The only AI platform that generates realistic, exam-caliber questions for any Brazilian certification or concurso — not generic flashcards, but practice that mirrors the real thing.

## Conversion & proof

- **Primary CTA:** Start for free — get immediate value, no commitment required.
- **Secondary fallback:** See how it works / Explore an example certification — for visitors who want proof before signing up.
- **The line a visitor remembers after 10 seconds:** Practice questions that feel like the real exam, for any certification you're preparing for.
- **Belief ladder:**
  1. Generic study apps don't prepare you for the actual exam format.
  2. AI can generate questions calibrated to a specific certification's depth and style.
  3. CertifiqueAI already covers your certification — IT, healthcare, law, engineering, or concurso público.
  4. Explaining why each option is right or wrong is more valuable than just marking correct/incorrect.
  5. Getting started is free and takes under two minutes.
- **Proof on hand:** none yet — placeholder for testimonials, press, or certification logos when available.

## Brand Personality

**Smart, clear, encouraging.** CertifiqueAI is knowledgeable without being intimidating. It speaks like the brilliant friend who has already passed the exam: direct, plain-language guidance with no condescension. The tone is confident and warm — it believes the user will pass, and every interaction reinforces that. Not a drill sergeant, not a cheerleader. An expert ally.

## Anti-references

- **Duolingo / gamified apps:** no streaks, no badges, no cartoon mascots, no XP bars, no "daily challenge" FOMO loops. This is serious professional preparation.
- **Generic SaaS dashboards:** no cream/near-white warm backgrounds, no gradient hero cards, no floating metric tiles, no "platform" energy. It should feel like a tool built for this specific purpose, not a template.
- **Google Classroom / LMS portals:** not institutional, not schoolish, not bureaucratic. No heavy sidebar navigation trees, no module-based content hierarchies. The experience is lean and direct.

## Design Principles

**Evidence beats assertion.** Every feature surfaces proof that the practice is working — scores, coverage, per-option explanations. Never make the user guess whether they're improving.

**Respect the session.** Users have limited time. The interface gets out of the way: no onboarding carousels, no upsell overlays mid-study, no decorative chrome that competes with content.

**Intelligent, not clinical.** The product is powered by AI but shouldn't feel sterile. Warmth comes from clarity and encouragement in copy, not decoration. Complexity is absorbed by the system so the user sees only what matters.

**Domain-agnostic confidence.** Whether the user is studying for AWS, OAB, CRM, or a municipal concurso — the interface treats every domain as worthy and first-class. No "IT-first" bias in the visual language.

**Earn trust through quality.** The questions have to feel authentic. The explanations have to be substantive. Design supports this by making the quality visible — clear typography, well-structured answer reviews, no visual noise competing with the content.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG 2.1 AA minimum. High-contrast text/background ratios, keyboard navigability throughout the quiz flow, screen reader support on interactive elements (radio buttons, result cards, modal dialogs). Reduced motion support required for all animations.
